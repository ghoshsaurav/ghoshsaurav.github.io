I am **Saurav Ghosh**, a PhD student in Computer Science at [Washington University in St. Louis](https://wustl.edu/). My research interests include **Human-Centered AI, Scientific Visualization, Uncertainty Quantification, Signal Analysis, Quantum Computing, and Decentralized Systems**.

I am currently a **Graduate Research Assistant in the [VIBE Lab](https://visualdata.wustl.edu/)**, where I work with Prof. [Alvitta Ottley](https://engineering.washu.edu/faculty/Alvitta-Ottley.html) on uncertainty-aware peak detection and visual analytics for GC-MS signal analysis. This work contributes to a Waters Corporation–funded research project on the proprietary ApexTrack peak detection algorithm.

I have also worked as a **Graduate Research Fellow in the [Jain Lab](https://www.cse.wustl.edu/~jain/)** with Prof. [Raj Jain](https://www.cse.wustl.edu/~jain/). My work focused on analytical frameworks for quantum-secured communication systems, including QKD, QPKS, and QDS, with emphasis on physical, link, and network-level vulnerabilities.

Before joining WashU, I completed my **M.S. in Applied Computer Science** at [Southeast Missouri State University](https://semo.edu/) with a 4.0/4.0 GPA. My thesis, titled *Access Control in P2P Network: A Blockchain Based Approach*, focused on blockchain-based access control for peer-to-peer and IoT environments. The thesis is available through [ProQuest](https://www.proquest.com/docview/3176153023).

I earned my **B.S. in Computer Science** from [BRAC University](https://www.bracu.ac.bd/) in Bangladesh. My undergraduate thesis explored prostate cancer detection using deep learning and transfer learning. The thesis is available through the [BRAC University Institutional Repository](http://hdl.handle.net/10361/16454).
