I am a PhD student in Computer Science at [Washington University in St. Louis](https://wustl.edu/). Currently, I am a Graduate Research Assistant in the [VIBE Lab](https://visualdata.wustl.edu/), where I work with Prof. [Alvitta Ottley](https://engineering.washu.edu/faculty/Alvitta-Ottley.html) on a research project connected to [Waters Corporation](https://www.waters.com/) ([NYSE: WAT](https://ir.waters.com/)), focused on improving the company’s proprietary ApexTrack algorithm. My current project is on uncertainty-aware peak detection for GC-MS/chromatographic data, problems where algorithms, uncertainty, and human judgment meet, with the goal of helping scientists understand when an algorithm’s output is stable or uncertain. In the past, I have also worked with Prof. [Raj Jain](https://www.cse.wustl.edu/~jain/) on quantum cryptographic primitives and secure networked systems.

Before starting my PhD, I completed my Master’s degree in Applied Computer Science at [Southeast Missouri State University](https://semo.edu/), where I worked closely with Prof. [Reshmi Mitra](https://semo.edu/people-directory/faculty-staff/mitra-reshmi.html). I worked on system and network security, blockchain, IoT, and decentralized networks. My [thesis](https://www.proquest.com/docview/3176153023) proposed a blockchain-based access control framework for peer-to-peer systems. During my master’s program, I received the Outstanding MS in Applied Computer Science Award, given to one graduating student per academic year in recognition of academic excellence and contributions within the program cohort.

I earned my Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science from [BRAC University](https://www.bracu.ac.bd/) in Bangladesh, where I worked with Dr. [Md. Golam Rabiul Alam](https://www.bracu.ac.bd/about/people/md-golam-rabiul-alam-phd). My undergraduate [thesis](http://hdl.handle.net/10361/16454) explored prostate cancer detection using deep learning and transfer learning, which gave me early exposure to machine learning, computer vision, and applied research.

My work has been published in Q1 journals and at conferences, with publishers including Elsevier, ACM, and Springer Nature. I have also contributed to conference posters, oral presentations, and preprints. Outside of research, I am a Volunteer Mentor with the Code to PhD Mentorship Program, where I support underrepresented and international students applying to PhD programs.

Over the years, I've worked across different branches of Computer Science, partly because studying and working across diverse academic and cultural environments exposed me to a range of problems I wanted to contribute to.
